Race Preperation

  1. You have  trained hard so far, with slow long  mileages, race paces, speed workout, etc... We trust all of you have been regular with your strength training sessions as much as your run workout sessions.

  2. A few pointers that can have additional focus going forward , especially during long Runs: 

    1. Plan Hydration the day before the long run, and follow the one that suits you, consistently for next two months (enerzal, orsl, bcaa etc...)

    2. Try out what kind of food suits you on the night prior to your long runs (practice for feeling your main race day) and in the morning of the long run

    3. Try out different hydration strategies and check what’s best suited to keep you hydrated and energetic all  through the run... fast n up - activate, reload, gels enerzal etc? 

    4. Try what kind of food suits you during the run... banana, biscuits, Chikki, dry fruits like figs/ dates, raisins, dry plum, cranberries etc... 

    5. Check how you will be carrying your nutrition / gels etc during the run/ race, though there will be ample aid stations… 

      We recall Deepa Bhat, an avid Ultra Runner mentioning, “The mark of a true ultra runner, is how well one can self support their runs” .

      Experiment with belts, pouches, pockets of shorts etc...

  3. Let’s not over-burden the one single lonely ‘Rest Day’ to do all your recovery for you. Let us support the ‘Rest Day’ actively. :) Remember to practice getting in at least 8 hours of sleep everyday, a bare minimum of 7 hours. That helps you recover from the strenuous training you have been undergoing for the Beyond 42.195 run.

    1. Start/Finish point: CRR College, Opposite Carmelaram Railway Station
    2. What you already know:
      1. Single loop route (calls for more mental sturdiness)
      2. Mix of road ~90% and trail ~10%(choose your foot-gear appropriately)
      3. If it rains close to the event expect some of the roads and trails to be slushy
      4. Gross Elevation ~250mts across the 50 Kms
    3. The GPX file of the route will be shared with you 4 days prior to the race day.

    1. Torch/Headlamp [M]You may drop off your headlamps at aid station No. 3 @ 14th Km. We will have it moved to the end point and you can collect it from the baggage counter.     3. Comfortable T-shirt & shorts/pants [M] 
    4. Photocopy of a valid identity proof [M]
    5. Mobile phone for contact [M]
    6. Hydration pack/waist belt with half litre drinking water/ reload/ enerzal [R]
    7. Fruit, energy bar, gels [R]
    8. Electrolyte powder/tablet [R]
    9. Emergency cash like Rs. 200 [R]
    10. GPS watch/device with .gpx file of the route preloaded in it [R]
    11. Extra change of clothes post run [R]
    12. Trail running shoes & socks [R]

    14. Sunglasses [R]

The Race

  1. Race Date: 31 October 2021
  2. Start Time: The race flag off time is 4:30AM
  3. Reporting Time: The runner is expected to report 1 hour / 60 mins before the race flag off time, i.e. 03:30 AM
  4. Mandatory Runner registrations: Ensure you register yourself with your allotted bib number at the runner registration desk. This will be near the baggage drop counter.
  5. There will be a baggage counter in case you wish to drop off any bag you are carrying with you. We however recommend you not to have valuables in them. Runner registrations and Baggage Drop are at the same counter.
  6. Runner registrations: Ensure you register yourself with your allotted bib number at the runner registration desk. This will be near the baggage counter.
  7. Race Briefing at 4:00 where we will inform you about the routes, route markers etc. It is mandatory to attend this. 
  8. Parking: We have limited parking space available at the school premises which is on a first come basis and thus we highly insist to car-pool with fellow runners please. There may be paid parking spots available at the Carmelaram Station (opposite the location). You can also park after the railway crossing along the road. We encourage not to park along the road outside the school as it's a narrow road and will hamper traffic at later hours. There will be volunteers guiding you to parking spots. We request you to cooperate with the volunteers who will be guiding you.
  9. We are working hard to give you a great experience and keep a fond memory of your 50K run with Beyond 42.195. Do remember that this run is fondly created by a bunch of runners and all your volunteers are runners 
  10. What to expect on the course:
    1. Crossing a railway cross in your 1st km and 43rd km
    2. Road running for ~90% of the course with lovely countryside feel
    3. Trail running for ~10%  of the course and if we are blessed with no rains 4-5 days before the race day, you are going to love this. And if it rains, you still might like it based on what level of trail experience you are looking for :)
    4. Clear signages indicating your next Left/ Right turns. 
    5. A total of 16 Aid stations: 8 Major and 8 Mini aid stations enroute.
      1. Major Aid stations: Start/ End, 9th Km, 16th Km, 24th Km, 31st Km, 35.8th Km, 40.2th Km, 46.5th Km
      2. Mini Aid Stations: 4th Km, 14.7th Km, 20.4th Km, 28.2th Km, 33rd Km, 38.8th Km, 43.8th Km, 48.2th Km
    6. Aid stations will have:
      1. Fruits - Bananas, Oranges, Watermelon
      2. FnU Gels
      3. Parle-G
      4. Chikki
      5. Jaggery
      6. Bread and Jam
      7. Pastries
      8. Water
      9. FnU Electrolytes
      10. Salt
      11. Tender Coconut
      12. Coke/Sprite
    7. Port-a-potties at start/ finish points and at 9th and 31st kms
    8. There is a cut off at 36th Kms; which needs to be completed within 5hrs 30mins. If you do not complete your 36 kms run within 5hrs 30mins you will have to step aside at the 36th km aid station and will not be allowed to finish the race. Organizers will arrange your pick up and drop to the end point. Note: The cut off is to ensure your safety and continued support.
    9. In case of emergency, you can let the nearest aid station know and we will help with your immediate pickup
    10. Remember to wear your bibs to show clearly as there will be manual timekeeping as you cross every aid station and the finish line.
    11. Our Fabulous photographers - so do not forget to smile please!
  11. Please be extra conscious about NOT LITTERING. Note that Penalties will be levied if the runner is found to litter.
    1. Keep your used gel packets / any other empty paper/ plastic packets till you reach the nearest aid station and trash them in the bins easily accessible as you run
    2. Ensure you trash the paper cups after consuming water/ electrolyte in the trash can, a few metres ahead of the aid stations.

Post Race

  1. This is how your race finish may look like:
    1. You will finish the race with the victorious smile n immense adrenaline rush at the finish line and ofcourse give a zealous strong-finish pose for our photographer as you cross the finish line
    2. You will collect your Finisher medal and celebrate thereafter
    3. Get your stretches done and take care of your major aches and pains from the 50K that you just ran, with our professional physiotherapists 
    4. Change into fresh pair of cotton clothes (recommended)
    5. Enjoy your fresh hot breakfast
    6. Take many pics with your Finisher Medal
    7. Chat about your route experience with co-runners, family and other runner friends
    8. AND remember to have your masks ON almost immediately after run
  2. You will receive your finisher certificate in your emails
  3. You will also know where you can access your race pictures. Isn't that what we all runners wait for? ;)
  4. You will receive a feedback form where we would love to get constructive feedback about the race
